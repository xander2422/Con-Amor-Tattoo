Common Questions

FAQ

Does Getting a Tattoo Hurt?

+
Pain varies depending on placement, size, and your personal tolerance. Some areas like ribs, spine, and inner arm tend to be more sensitive, while outer arms and thighs are generally easier to sit through. Most people describe the feeling as a consistent scratching or vibrating sensation. Your artist will work with you to make sure you're as comfortable as possible throughout the session.

Do You Use Numbing Cream?

+
We don't apply numbing cream at the studio, but you're welcome to use a topical numbing product before your appointment. If you do, apply it about 30 to 45 minutes before your session and cover the area with plastic wrap. Keep in mind that some numbing agents can affect the skin's texture, so check with your artist first if you have questions about a specific product.

Deposits & Cancellations

+
A deposit may be required to secure your appointment. Deposits are non-refundable but will be applied toward your tattoo. Please give at least 48 hours notice if you need to reschedule. No-shows will forfeit their deposit. Contact your artist directly to discuss specific deposit requirements.

What Should I Do Before My Session?

+
Arrive on time and well-rested. Eat a good meal before your appointment and stay hydrated. Wear comfortable clothing that allows easy access to the tattoo area. Avoid alcohol and blood-thinning medications for at least 24 hours before your session. Your artist will review the design with you and make any adjustments before starting.

How Much Does a Tattoo Cost?

+
Pricing varies by size, detail, placement, and artist. Walk-in flash designs start at $60. Custom pieces are quoted on a case-by-case basis. DM your artist or email us with your idea for a quote. We accept cash and all major cards.

Aftercare Instructions

+
Keep your bandage on for 2-4 hours after your session. Wash gently with lukewarm water and fragrance-free soap. Pat dry and apply a thin layer of unscented moisturizer or aftercare ointment. Avoid direct sunlight, pools, and soaking for at least 2 weeks. Don't pick or scratch the healing tattoo. Your artist will provide personalized aftercare advice based on the piece.

What to Expect

+
Arrive on time and well-rested. Eat a good meal before your appointment. Wear comfortable clothing that allows easy access to the tattoo area. Your artist will review the design with you, make any adjustments, and prepare the stencil before starting. Sessions vary in length depending on the piece. Your artist will give you a time estimate beforehand.

Age & ID Requirements

+
You must be 18 years or older with a valid government-issued photo ID. No exceptions. Expired IDs are not accepted. If you look under 30, expect to be asked for identification.

Deposits & Cancellations

+
A deposit may be required to secure your appointment. Deposits are non-refundable but will be applied toward your tattoo. Please give at least 48 hours notice if you need to reschedule. No-shows will forfeit their deposit. Contact your artist directly to discuss specific deposit requirements.

Pricing

+
Pricing varies by size, detail, placement, and artist. Walk-in flash designs start at $60. Custom pieces are quoted on a case-by-case basis. DM your artist or email us with your idea for a quote. We accept cash and all major cards.

Walk-In Wednesdays
